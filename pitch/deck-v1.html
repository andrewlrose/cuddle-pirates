Key guarantee

Nothing moves to the next stage until it’s approved. No “we used the wrong version” at comp.

Stage 2 of 6

RIG

INGEST→ RIG→ ANIM→ FX→ COMP→ EXPORT

What it does: Approved concept → 3D. MJ concept → Meshy base mesh → Blender cleanup → custom UE5 stylized rig.

Sir Fuzzy Drake: MJ captain concept → Meshy 3D base → Blender proportion correction → UE5 custom rig.
Stylized-first: plush proportions, huge round eyes, cloth pajama simulation throughout.

No MetaHuman dependency

Hero characters are fully custom. MetaHuman is a fallback seam only for photoreal sub-assets. The artist owns the shape.

Cloth sim baked at rig

Pajama fabric, captain’s coat, The Fluffy Dutchman’s pillowcase hull — all sim’d at rig stage. ANIM inherits. No re-bake on iteration.

Stage 3 of 6

ANIM

INGEST→ RIG→ ANIM→ FX→ COMP→ EXPORT

What it does: Hero rig → final performance. Physical comedy timing, silent heist sequences, cloth interaction with environment.

Infiltration sequence:
• Freeze-against-wall: full-body ragdoll physics response to a simulated sound event
• Ragdoll acrobatics on the bed: silent comedy — performance is entirely physical
• The bow: the Pirates know they’re performing. The animation sells the theatricality.

Key: ANIM inherits cloth sim from RIG. No re-simulation at this stage. Iterate on performance without re-baking fabric. Notes are same-day.

Stage 4 of 6

FX

INGEST→ RIG→ ANIM→ FX→ COMP→ EXPORT

What it does: Soft-body and cloth simulation on hero assets in environment. Secondary FX: dust motes, light shafts, practical in-world effects.

Cuddle Pirates example:
• The Fluffy Dutchman’s pillowcase hull: cloth sim on a moving prop, legs driving contact underneath
• Pajama sleeves catching air during acrobatics
• The Dutchman’s rigging: rope and sail physics as the ship maneuvers

Scoped deliberately: No VDB explosions. No rigid body debris. Everything is cloth and soft-body — well-understood, controllable, iteration-friendly. FX does not blow up the schedule.

Stage 5 of 6

COMP

INGEST→ RIG→ ANIM→ FX→ COMP→ EXPORT

What it does: Environment + character + FX → final image. Bedroom lighting rig, atmosphere, two competing light sources that drive the comedy.

Two light sources as story devices:
• Warm hall light (threat) — orange, hard-edged, moves when Mom is moving
• Cool window moonlight (safe) — blue-grey, soft, always present

The freeze-against-wall beat is a lighting cut. Warm light creeps into frame → Pirates freeze. Light retreats → they exhale.

All COMP in UE5. Handcrafted lookdev — not procedural generation.

Stage 6 of 6

EXPORT

INGEST→ RIG→ ANIM→ FX→ COMP→ EXPORT

What it does: UE5 → editorial-ready delivery. Four lanes. Always-on.

OutputFormatNotes
4K master framesEXR sequenceFull-float, comp-ready, re-deliverable to any future spec
MP4 dailiesH.264 / H.265Review-ready, same-day, no transcode step
Avid round-tripAAF + audio stemsTimecoded, multi-track, drops into Media Composer
DaVinci round-tripXML + audio stemsTimeline-accurate, color-managed for Resolve

Volta doesn’t ask which NLE you use. All four formats ship on every export. Changing tools mid-production is a config switch, not a re-pipeline.
Audio stems: dialogue / score / SFX delivered discretely. Full separation from day one.

Ask the editor their NLE before the pitch if possible. Either way, the deck covers both. "We built Avid and DaVinci lanes. You tell us which lane you drive and we hand you the keys."
