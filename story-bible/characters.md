## THE CORE FIVE

The show has a deliberate **2 + 3 structure**:
- **2 leadership roles:** Captain + First Mate (drive the mission)
- **3 grifter/performer pirates:** the physical comedy engine

All five are **plush toys in fuzzy pirate pajamas** — caricatures of historical
pirates/explorers. Designs based on caricatures of Raylan's uncles and grandpas.

---

### 1. Sir Fuzzy Drake — *The Captain*

**Historical basis:** Sir Francis Drake (English)
**MJ reference:** Yes — 4 images (see inventory.json)
**Title:** Captain of The Fluffy Dutchman. Leader of the Cuddle Pirates.

**Core traits:** Knowledgeable, determined, heroic, capable. Boisterous personality
given to exaggeration. Launches into overly dramatic monologues about their quests.

**The flaw:** Driven by the thrill of adventure and danger to the point that he is
distracted by new adventures *before* even taking possession of the treasures of
his conquests. Like a video game player who starts a new game before finishing the
last one — the next conquest is always more exciting than the current one.

**The brooding note:** Has a brooding side, possibly because his mother was the
princess from *The Princess and the Pea* fable.

**The anchor:** Has an abiding loyalty to Anne Bunny. She is the only one who can
re-focus him when he goes off chasing a new distraction.

**Archetype:** J. Peterman (*Seinfeld*) — not a dumb stereotype, but definitively
flawed by the drive toward the *next* thing. Heroic and capable; just perpetually
distracted by the horizon.

**Signature move:** Dramatic monologue about the quest. The crew has heard all of
them. Anne Bunny can cut one short with a look.

**Physical description:** A strong and strapping teddy bear. Majestic captain's
hat. Billowing pirate garments. Finely groomed beard.

**Dialogue style:** Overly formal regardless of the situation. Can switch without
warning to playful childish quips or feigned outrage when contradicted or
challenged by Anne Bunny.

---

### 2. Anne Bunny — *The First Mate*

**Historical basis:** Anne Bonny (Irish-American privateer)
**MJ reference:** Yes — 4 images ("Bunny Pirate" set, see inventory.json)
**Title:** First Mate of The Fluffy Dutchman. American privateer.

**Core traits:** Stage director personality. Driven. Gets the mission done.

**Motivation:** She LOVES the Cuddle Pirates' epic pillow fights back home on
Puff-tooga. The whole point of every mission is to come home with enough fluffy
plunder to have a legendary pillow fight. The pillows are the prize.

**The flaw:** Her iron determination is constantly undermined by her own compassion.
When she actually meets the children and hears their struggles to fall asleep, she
can't help but care. The mission softens. She gets aggravated by Sir Fuzzy Drake's
distractions AND by her own empathy pulling her off-course.

**Relationship with Sir Fuzzy Drake:** She is the only one who can re-focus him.
A single look or word from her cuts through any dramatic monologue.

**Archetype:** Winston Zeddemore (*Ghostbusters*) — the straight man. Not the
flashiest character, but the most reliable. The crew would fall apart without her.

**Physical description:** Practical pirate clothing built for adventuring, with
an eye for utility and just a touch of femininity. A soft, fluffy tail that she
constantly twitches when she is deep in thought — the tail is her tell.

**Dialogue style:** Formal and straightforward, mission-focused. Under pressure
she becomes argumentative and tensely confrontational. She does not raise her
voice — she gets colder.

---

### 3. Jean LaFluff — *The Charming Grifter*

**Historical basis:** Jean Lafitte (French privateer)
**Title:** Grifter/performer pirate. No formal rank — he operates on charm.

**Core traits:** Refined gentleman. Charming renegade. Classic grifter type.
Sharp, clever, well-dressed by pirate standards. Aristocratic mannerisms and
dress beyond what the crew requires.

**The tendency:** Always temporarily drops the mission to:
- Hang out with the teddy bears (playing poker — à la Cassius Marcellus Coolidge
  *Dogs Playing Poker* paintings)
- Romance the cute dolls he encounters during adventures
- Simply enjoy wherever he is before he remembers he's supposed to be on a heist

**The loyalty:** Always comes back. He will absolutely abandon his newest doll
girlfriend mid-conversation to rescue a crewmate. No questions asked.

**Key skill:** Distracting dogs with poker games. This is a recurring bit — dogs
can always be managed if Jean LaFluff deals the cards.

**Archetype:** Face (*The A-Team*) — charming renegade, can talk his way in or
out of anything. Vanity can be over the top (à la Johnny Bravo) but he is NOT
stupid — the vanity is performance, the intelligence is real.

**Physical description:** Tall and slender teddy bear. A well-groomed mustache.
A permanent twinkle in his eye. Always the best-dressed pirate in any room.

**Dialogue style:** Not specified in source documents — TBD.

**Historical notes (from Lafitte research):**
> *"Sharp and resourceful, but also handsome and friendly, enjoying drinking,
> gambling, and women. He was known to adopt more aristocratic mannerisms and
> dress than most of his fellow privateers."*
> *"Known for treating captive crew members well."*

### 4. Vasco Pajama — *The Inventor-Navigator*

**Historical basis:** Vasco da Gama (Portuguese)
**Title:** Navigator of The Fluffy Dutchman.

**Core traits:** Intellectual of the highest order — the Leonardo da Vinci of
the crew. A tinkerer who is always inventing new gadgets.

**The skill set:** Masterfully uses the ropes, pulleys, and sails from The Fluffy
Dutchman to devise ingenious traps and pillow-fighting contraptions. Also adept
at creating illusions and diversionary spectacles that entertain children while
the actual pillow heist is underway. The show's technical genius.

**The flaw:** Imposter Complex. He secretly believes he is a fraud despite clear
evidence to the contrary. This surfaces as comically angry outbursts whenever his
traps and gadgets are foiled — or accidentally destroyed by Marco Pillow.

**Relationship with Marco Pillow:** Classic inventor/foil comedy pair. Marco is
his biggest supporter AND his biggest source of unintentional disaster.

**Archetype:** The genius who doubts himself. Da Vinci energy, but with a hair
trigger for indignant rage when the contraption fails.

**Physical description:** Small and slender teddy bear. Round spectacles perched
on his nose. A perpetually furrowed brow — he is always running calculations.

**Dialogue style:** Not specified in source documents — TBD.

**Historical quote resonance:**
> *"I am not afraid of the darkness. Real death is preferable to a life without
> living."* — Vasco da Gama

---

### 5. Marco Pillow — *The Gentle Saboteur*

**Historical basis:** Marco Polo (Italian)
**Title:** Second Mate of The Fluffy Dutchman.

**Core traits:** Kind. Jovial. Prone to boisterous outbursts of laughter. Warm
and genuinely supportive — always willing to offer advice and encouragement to
children in need.

**The role:** The foil to Vasco Pajama. He either:
1. Immediately identifies the fatal flaw in Vasco's contraptions (out loud, in
   front of everyone, with complete innocence), OR
2. Accidentally breaks the contraption *just before it was about to succeed*

His destruction is never malicious — he is always trying to help. This makes it
worse. And he is always genuinely sorry.

**The counterweight:** Marco is also Vasco's most sincere cheerleader. He is the
one who tells Vasco he's not a fraud and that his ingenuity is the crew's greatest
asset. He means it every time, even when he's standing in the wreckage.

**Archetype:** The kind-hearted bumbler. Not stupid — just larger than the situation
requires. The crew loves him unconditionally.

**Physical description:** Rotund teddy bear. A bright red bandana around his neck.
A wide, infectious smile that he deploys constantly and sincerely.

**Dialogue style:** Not specified in source documents — TBD.

**Historical quote resonance:**
> *"I did not write half of what I saw, for I was always consumed by the wonder
> of it all."* — Marco Polo
