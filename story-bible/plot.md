## Per-Episode Structure — Cuddle Pirate Theater

1. **Infiltrate** — Pirates arrive at the bedroom (window, under door, etc.)
2. **Dodge** — Parent is nearby. Pirates evade detection. Physical comedy engine.
3. **Perform** — Acrobatic ragdoll antics for the child. The show within the show.
4. **Connect** — Child reveals something (worry, sadness, question). Juniper: missing sock.
5. **Story** — Pirates tell a bedtime story. Animated as a nested sub-story.
6. **Heist** — Once child (and parent) are asleep: steal the pillows and blankets.
7. **Escape** — Into The Fluffy Dutchman (legs out the bottom). Sail to Puff-tooga.

## Pilot Episode — Juniper's Bedroom

- **Who:** The Cuddle Pirates (2–3 for the pilot — hero cast TBD)
- **Where:** Juniper's bedroom, nighttime
- **Parent:** Mom — across the hall doing laundry

### Inciting Incident (the WHY — from docx)

The Cuddle Pirates' pillow fort on Puff-tooga was *destroyed in an epic pillow
fight.* To rebuild the fort walls, they need a **perfectly shaped stuffy** —
and they have intelligence that one exists in Juniper's room. That's why they're
there. This is the driving mission of the pilot.

### The Complication — Sock-topuss

Juniper is sad about her **missing sock.** The sock was taken by the dog.
In the hypnagogic state, the dog *manifests as* **Sock-topuss** — a goofy
creature obsessed with covering its tentacles with colorful socks.

> **Reconciliation note:** The docx says "the dog IS the Socktopuss monster."
> The Outline gives Sock-topuss independent anatomy (tentacles) and a
> sympathetic motivation (it collects socks to feel less lonely and more colorful).
> These are compatible: the real dog took the sock; Sock-topuss is its
> hypnagogic form — what Juniper's waking dream makes of the dog. Jean LaFluff
> can distract the real dog with poker while the crew deals with the manifestation.

The crew must:

1. Find Sock-topuss without angering it (it is goofy and unpredictable, not evil)
2. Discover that it collects socks because it is lonely and wants to be colorful
3. Negotiate an alternative solution — help Sock-topuss find a new hobby
4. Recover Juniper's sock through persuasion, not force

Sock-topuss gets a redemption arc. The confrontation ends in negotiation,
not combat. This is the show's emotional register — even the monster has feelings.

### Narrative Mechanism — The Hypnagogic State

Juniper can see the Pirates because she is in the **hypnagogic state** —
the transitional period between wakefulness and sleep, when reality begins to
warp and conscious thought dissolves into early dreaming. During this state,
hypnagogic hallucinations are common: involuntary imagined experiences, sometimes
called "waking dreams."

This is the in-world rule: **adults are fully awake and cannot see the Pirates.**
Children in the hypnagogic state can. The Pirates time their visits carefully
because the window closes once the child fully falls asleep.
